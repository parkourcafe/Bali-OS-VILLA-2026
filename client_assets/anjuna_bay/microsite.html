What is genuinely working

Credit where due

The on-ground team earns its scores: Booking guests rate Staff 9.5/10, a named contact ("Budi") sends a welcome WhatsApp the day before arrival, and reviews call the team "responsive" and "available 24/7" — with zero indexed complaints about unreachability. Cleanliness, design and the new build are consistently praised. The channel base is modern too: one Guesty backend feeding Airbnb, Booking, Agoda, Trip.com/Ctrip and Marriott Homes & Villas. This audit is not about weak hospitality — hospitality is the strongest asset. It is about the commercial layer: inventory, pricing, brand and distribution.

The leak map — adversarially verified

Why bookings are few: seven leaks, ranked

1

Inventory cap — the root cause CONFIRMED

Only ~9 identifiable villas (A3, B1, B2, B3, B10, 2A1, 2A5, A14, A15) are live across all channels — a ceiling of 9–15 bookable units out of ~69 planned. Aurora (32) is made-to-order/off-plan, Palms (15 townhouses, due Jun 2026) has zero rental presence anywhere, Urbana (22) completes Dec 2026 — and even within the finished cluster, 10–16 apparently completed villas are simply not listed. Booking volume is structurally capped: there is little to book.

Evidence: exhaustive listing census across Airbnb/Booking/Agoda/Trip/Marriott/Guesty · FazWaz & asia.villas project statuses
2

Rate leakage VERIFIED-PLAUSIBLE

The $144 2BR lead-in is a real (non-refundable) rate; 1BRs show from $66. That is 33–44% below segment medians despite 4.87–5.0★ quality. Even at 85% occupancy this earns ~$122 per available night — mere parity with the average Uluwatu listing ($130 RevPAR). Estimated foregone revenue: ~$9–35k gross per month across the ~10 listed units.

Evidence: Booking/Agoda rate snippets · airroi Uluwatu: median $216, 2–3BR ADR ~$259, top-25% $313+ · no dynamic-pricing tooling traceable
3

No demand engine VERIFIED-PLAUSIBLE

Guest demand is rented, not generated. @anjunabay markets real-estate sales; the operator's own channels target villa owners, not guests — and its site copy tells guests to "book through Airbnb for the best rates". No Google Business profile, no rental ads found, SEO ≈ 5 indexed pages. New listings start from zero with nothing pushing demand to them.

Evidence: IG bios & content review · site index census · Ad Library probes (403-limited)
4

Fragmented review equity VERIFIED-PLAUSIBLE

Social proof is split across 12+ separate listings, so most units present as "new — 4–13 reviews" while B1 alone holds 86. The flagship Booking listing scores 8.7 — below the "Wonderful 9+" filter quality-conscious guests apply. Every newly-added unit relaunches from zero.

Evidence: per-listing review census (B1 86 · A14 44 · 2BR 6 · Booking aggregate 59)
5

Thin, inconsistent distribution

Five channels only — and 5 of the 9 live units sit on exactly one channel each (B10 only on Booking, B3 only on Agoda, A14/A15/B1 only on Airbnb). Absent entirely: Vrbo/Expedia, TripAdvisor, Google Vacation Rentals, metasearch, luxury agencies, retreat platforms. The direct site has one Anjuna property indexed. The "Anjuna" name itself leaks brand searches to Anjuna, Goa (India).

Evidence: per-channel listing census · site: searches across 12 platforms
6

Listings that undersell

Unit codes ("Anjuna B10", "2A5") mean nothing to guests; near-identical titles cause choice paralysis; a live title typo and an Agoda URL/unit mismatch signal sloppy channel management; templated copy never pre-empts the noise objection — while reviews report a rock-breaker "all day, every day" and a nightclub until 3–4am, undisclosed.

Evidence: listing titles/snippets · Booking review texts (construction + Tabu club)
7

Owner flies blind

No public SLA, no management replies to reviews, no evidence of funnel-level owner reporting (live inventory → inquiries → response times → conversion → ADR vs market). When results are a black box, "few bookings" is a feeling with no lever attached.

Observation: no owner-reporting artefacts found · booking email is a Gmail address

What to do differently

Seven moves, in order of impact

  1. Put the inventory on the market. List every completed unit on every major channel and onboard Palms the day it hands over. This is the only move that multiplies the number of bookings — everything else improves their value.
  2. Install daily revenue management and reposition the 2BR at $210–300 with a guarded ladder; make the hook rate refundable; fix rate parity so direct is genuinely best.
  3. Build the guest-facing Anjuna Bay brand — landmark listing titles instead of unit codes, Google Business profile + review flow, guest content split from the sales brand, book-direct incentive.
  4. Consolidate review equity — restructure listings so social proof transfers to new units instead of every unit relaunching from zero.
  5. Widen distribution — Vrbo/Expedia, TripAdvisor, Google Vacation Rentals, metasearch, luxury agencies, retreat/group platforms; put every unit on every channel.
  6. Manage the noise objection honestly — disclose construction phase and nightlife in listing copy, mitigate on-site — protecting the 4.9★ asset through 2026.
  7. Unify first response + owner visibility — one AI layer over all channels (approved answers < 2 minutes, 24/7, qualification, D1/D3/D7 follow-up, human escalation) and a weekly owner funnel report. Results become numbers, not feelings.
Keep what works: the on-ground service team and standards (Staff 9.5), the Guesty stack and existing channel accounts, the welcome-WhatsApp ritual — and change the commercial layer around them without disrupting guests.
