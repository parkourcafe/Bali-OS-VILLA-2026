What is genuinely working

Credit where due

Distribution is wide — Airbnb (~10 unit listings), Booking.com, Agoda, Trip.com, Marriott Homes & Villas, plus a Guesty direct-booking site. This audit is not about weak hospitality. It is about economics and control.

The leak map

Six places the results leak

1

Rate leakage — the big one

Occupancy is being bought with below-median pricing. High occupancy at $144/night earns less per available night than healthy occupancy at the market rate.

Evidence: Booking.com "from $144" · airroi Uluwatu: median $216, 2BR ADR ~$260 @ 62% occ
2

OTA dependence

Nearly every booking arrives through an OTA — with a commission attached. The direct Guesty site exists but has no owned traffic feeding it.

Evidence: no confirmed rental ads, no owned demand channel found
3

Brand aimed at buyers, not guests

@anjunabay (3.5k followers) markets real estate sales. The rental product has no guest-facing brand, no Google Business profile, no remarketing.

Evidence: IG bio "Luxury Real Estate" · no Google/Tripadvisor rental profile found
4

First-response risk at scale

Inquiries land in 6+ inboxes at once. The operator manages ~207 listings and grew ~4× in a year — exactly where first-response speed breaks. Digital response time is unverified; concierge runs 18h/day, not 24/7.

To be measured: free Secret Guest Audit (one timed test)
5

Reputation surface gaps

Recurring review themes — nightclub noise, active construction, scooter access — are not pre-managed in listing copy. A 4.9★ asset is exposed as volume grows.

Evidence: indexed review snippets across Airbnb/Booking
6

Owner visibility

No evidence of a funnel-level owner report: inquiries → response times → conversion → rate decisions. When the funnel is invisible, results become a feeling instead of a number.

Observation: no public owner-reporting artefacts found

The same villas, run differently

Today

  • 6+ channels → separate inboxes (Airbnb, Booking, Agoda, Trip, Marriott, Guesty, WhatsApp)
  • Lean team scaling ~4×/yr answers when it can — speed unverified, tone varies
  • Demand bought with below-median rates; occupancy high, revenue capped

  • Owner sees outcomes, not the funnel

With a governed response layer

  • One intake over every channel — approved first reply < 2 minutes, 24/7, multilingual
  • Every inquiry qualified (dates, party, budget, add-ons) and logged; quiet leads followed up D1/D3/D7
  • Rates stepped toward market with a guarded ladder — revenue-led, not price-led
  • Rental brand stood up: Google profile, guest-facing content, direct CTA — OTA share falls
  • Owner gets a weekly funnel report: inquiries, response times, conversion, ADR vs market

What to do differently

Five moves

  1. Reprice toward market with a guarded rate ladder — protect an occupancy floor, capture the gap. Pricing stays with you; our reporting makes it visible weekly.
  2. Stand up the rental brand's owned demand — Google Business profile + review flow, guest-facing content split from the sales brand, a direct-booking CTA everywhere.
  3. Unify first response — one AI layer over all channels: approved answers in under 2 minutes, 24/7, multilingual, human escalation for VIP / pricing / complaints.
  4. Qualify and upsell systematically — structured capture, add-ons (transfers, chef, beach club), D1/D3/D7 follow-up on quiet leads.
  5. Weekly owner report — inquiries → response times → conversion → ADR vs market. Results become numbers.
