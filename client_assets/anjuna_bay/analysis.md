# Anjuna Bay — Rental Performance Analysis (public data)

Prepared for: the Anjuna Bay owner side. Current rental operator: **Balinest Villas** (Guesty).
Basis: `anjuna_effectiveness_audit.md` — public data only (OTA snippets, airbtics, airroi); site pages
block automated reads (403), so figures are search-index-based and confidence-flagged. Nothing here
is invented; estimates are labelled. Context note: owner dissatisfaction is client-provided context,
not something we assert publicly.

## 1. How the operation works today (facts)
- Distribution: ~10 Airbnb unit listings + Booking.com, Agoda, Trip.com, **Marriott Homes & Villas**, plus Guesty direct site (balinestvillas.guestybookings.com). Wide reach.
- Quality: Airbnb 4.87–5.0★ per unit (B1: 86 reviews; A14: 44), Booking 8.7/10 with **Staff 9.5**; Guest-favorite + Superhost badges. On-ground service is genuinely strong.
- Occupancy: high — ~5–7 reviews/mo per lead unit (est.), manager-claimed ~85%.
- Manager scale: Balinest ≈ **207 listings**, portfolio **+285% in 12 months** (airbtics) — a lean team growing ~4×/yr.
- Marketing: @anjunabay (3.5k followers) markets **real-estate sales**, not stays. No confirmed rental ads, no Google Business review profile for the rental brand, no owned direct-demand engine. Bookings ride OTA algorithms.
- Pricing: 2BR advertised **from $144/night** vs Uluwatu market median **~$216** and 2BR ADR **~$260 @ ~62% occ** (airroi).

## 2. Where the results leak (the 6 leaks)
1. **Rate leakage (largest, quantifiable).** Occupancy is being *bought* with below-median pricing. ESTIMATE per 2BR: today ≈ $144 × 85% ≈ **$122 revenue/night-equivalent**; market-median scenario ≈ $216 × 70% ≈ **$151** (+~24%); top-band $260 × 62% ≈ $161. High occupancy ≠ maximum owner revenue.
2. **OTA dependence.** Nearly all demand is OTA-sourced → 15–18% commissions on almost every booking; direct Guesty site has no owned traffic engine feeding it.
3. **Brand misalignment.** The only marketing asset (@anjunabay) targets property buyers. The rental product has no owned audience, no Google profile, no remarketing — invisible outside OTA search.
4. **First-response risk at scale.** Inquiries land in 6+ inboxes at once; digital response speed is unconfirmed (403), concierge is 18h/day not 24/7, and a team scaling 4×/yr is exactly where first-response latency breaks. To be measured via a Secret Guest Audit (one respectful test).
5. **Reputation surface gaps.** No Google/Tripadvisor profile for the rental operation; recurring review themes (nightclub noise, active construction, scooter access) are not pre-managed in listing copy — a 4.9★ score at risk as volume grows.
6. **Owner visibility.** No public evidence of a funnel-level owner report (inquiries → replies → conversions → rate decisions). When owners can't see the funnel, "results" become a black box — the classic root of dissatisfaction.

## 3. What should be done differently (5 moves)
1. **Reprice toward market** with a guarded rate ladder (protect occupancy floor, capture the ~24% gap). Pricing authority stays with the operator/owner — we surface the data weekly.
2. **Stand up the rental brand's owned demand**: Google Business profile + review flow, rental-focused IG/content split from the sales brand, direct-booking CTA everywhere.
3. **Unify first response**: one AI response layer over Airbnb/Booking/Agoda/Trip/Marriott/Guesty/WhatsApp — approved answers, <2-minute first reply on covered channels, 24/7, multilingual, with human escalation for VIP/pricing/complaints.
4. **Qualify + upsell systematically**: dates/party/budget/purpose capture, add-on offers (transfers, chef, beach-club), D1/D3/D7 follow-up on quiet leads.
5. **Weekly owner funnel report**: inquiries, response times, conversion, ADR vs market — so results are visible, not felt.

## 4. Fit with our offer
Moves 3–5 are exactly the **Villa Ops Response System** (14-day install). Moves 1–2 are recommendations the owner/operator executes; our layer makes them measurable.
