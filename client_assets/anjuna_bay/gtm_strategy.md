# Anjuna Surf & Sunset — Positioning, Pricing & Go-To-Market Plan

Synthesis of everything: metrics (`metrics_summary.md`), comp set (`comp_set.md`), scorecard, booking
journey (`booking_journey_map.md`), and demand research (`voice_of_traveler.md`). Every move is tied
to a verified finding. Staged, in priority order.

---

## 0. Positioning
**Who it's for:** couples/honeymooners + surfers first (the two segments that own Uluwatu's volume and
identity), then luxury and nomads.
**The promise:** *"Brand-new cliffside pool villas, a short walk from Thomas Beach — the view + the
private pool + the privacy that people come to Uluwatu for, without the Canggu crowds."*
**Why this wins:** travelers' top-3 loves are exactly cliff/sunset view, private pool, and quiet
privacy — Anjuna has all three but sells them as unit codes. Positioning = say what they already want.
**Guardrail (from the data):** the "Uluwatu is becoming the next Canggu" sentiment + Anjuna's own
on-site construction is a live risk — lean into "quiet, private, walk to the beach" and disclose/manage
construction honestly, don't hide it.

## 1. Own website + Google — YES, and why
**Own website: YES — it's the biggest single gap.** Anjuna's direct channel is the *weakest in the
comp set* (Guesty, 1 unit indexed), while COZ, NOUR, Zyloh, The Young Villas, Uluwatu Surf Villas,
Bingin Castaways all run their own sites/engines. Direct booking saves the 15–18% OTA commission and
builds a guest database. You already have Guesty — a proper booking site is a config + design job, not
new tech.
- Fast, mobile-first, real-time availability (Guesty engine), one page per villa with landmark titles.
- **Book-direct incentive** (the lever every strong competitor uses): "best rate here + free airport
  transfer / floating breakfast" — directly answers the market's #1 complaint (transport/scooter).
- WhatsApp button on every page (Uluwatu guests *expect* the manager's WhatsApp — confirmed norm).

**Google — the cheapest high-ROI wins first:**
- **Google Business Profile: create it (it does not exist).** This is a free, missing asset — puts the
  villas on Google Maps + the map-pack where "villas in Uluwatu" searchers look (Stage 3 of the journey).
- **Google Vacation Rentals / free booking links:** list the direct site so it shows in Google Hotels
  with a "free" direct link next to the OTAs — commission-free demand.
- **Google Ads: YES but small and targeted, AFTER the profile + site.** Two campaigns only:
  (a) **brand defense** — bid on "Anjuna Bay villa" so you own your own name (cheap, high intent);
  (b) a tight "Uluwatu private pool villa" / "villa near Thomas Beach" search campaign, geo-targeted to
  AU/EU, pointed at the direct site. Don't spray budget — Anjuna's demand is OTA-algorithm-driven today;
  ads are to build the *direct* funnel, not replace OTAs.

## 2. Own advertising (paid social)
**YES — Meta/Instagram + TikTok, small budget, tightly targeted.** This is the Inspiration stage
(30–180 days out) where Anjuna currently has zero presence.
- **Creative = the top-3 loves:** cliff/sunset view, the private pool, walk-to-Thomas-Beach. Short
  reels of the pool at sunset outperform any text.
- **Targeting:** Australia first (dominant origin), then EU couples/honeymooners and surfers; interest
  = Bali/Uluwatu/surf/honeymoon. Retarget site + IG visitors.
- **Timing to booking windows:** AU 30–90d, EU/honeymooners 60–180d — run always-on but weight spend
  2–6 months before AU/EU high season.
- Split the rental brand from @anjunabay (which sells real estate) — guests and buyers are different
  audiences; a guest-facing handle is required.

## 3. SEO — YES, but paired with ads (it's slow)
- **Local SEO first** (fastest, and totally missing): Google Business Profile + consistent
  name/address/phone + reviews flow. This is where "villa Uluwatu" map searches convert.
- **On-site SEO:** one indexable page per villa with landmark, benefit-rich titles ("2BR Cliffside Pool
  Villa, 5-min walk to Thomas Beach") — not unit codes; fix the current 5-page thin site + the title
  typo.
- **Content SEO (medium-term):** a small blog owning long-tail the segments search — "where to stay in
  Uluwatu for surfers", "Uluwatu villa with private pool near Padang Padang", "honeymoon villa Uluwatu".
  This is how Uluwatu Surf Villas / Cabo Bali capture research-first guests.
- Reality check: SEO pays off in months → use Google Business + ads to bridge the gap now.

## 4. Links (link strategy)
- **Link-in-bio (Linktree):** IG/TikTok → direct booking engine + WhatsApp + Google reviews. Every
  strong competitor (Ayora, Marghe, Young Villas) runs one; Anjuna has none.
- **Consistent listing links & naming across OTAs** — same landmark name, same photos everywhere (fixes
  the cross-channel-consistency gap; today B10-only-Booking, B3-only-Agoda etc.).
- **Backlinks / earned placement:** get the villas into the "best villas in Uluwatu / Bingin" roundups
  and surf-travel resellers (Perfect Wave, The Perfect Hideaway) — this is Funnel D, where research-first
  guests decide. Partner links: Canna Beach Club, surf schools, wedding/event planners.
- **Google Business + review links** on the site and in post-stay follow-ups (builds the missing
  Google review base).

## 6. Pricing recommendation
Today: **2BR $144 non-refundable · 1BR $66** — near the bottom of the set, cheapest terms. Market:
median ADR **$216**, 2–3BR ADR **$259**, RevPAR $130, top-25% $313+.
- **Reprice with a guarded ladder** (protect an occupancy floor, capture the gap):
  - 1BR: from $66 → **$110–140**
  - 2BR: from $144 → **$190–240 base**, seasonal peak **$280+**
  - 3BR: → **$280–350**
- **Add a free-cancellation rate** alongside a cheaper non-refundable one (rivals win on flexibility).
- **Fix rate parity** — the direct site must be the best price (+ a perk), not "book on Airbnb for best
  rate" (their current, self-defeating message).
- **Daily dynamic pricing** (PriceLabs-class). Benchmark: Lago Villas (Bingin, same Guesty stack) runs
  92% occupancy at **+48% RevPAR vs market** with per-villa branding + daily revenue management.
- Expected effect (estimate, labelled): repricing toward median lifts RevPAR from ~$122 to ~$150–160
  per available 2BR night without killing occupancy — the ~24% gap we quantified.

## 7. What to improve from what exists — priority order
1. **List ALL completed inventory** on all major channels; onboard Palms on handover. *(Multiplies
   booking count — the #1 lever.)*
2. **Rename every listing** to landmark, benefit-rich titles; kill unit codes; fix the title typo and
   Agoda mismatch.
3. **Reprice + free-cancellation + parity + dynamic pricing.** *(Captures the ~24% rate gap.)*
4. **Create the Google Business Profile + own booking site + WhatsApp + book-direct incentive.**
   *(Closes the weakest-in-set direct-booking gap.)*
5. **Consolidate review equity** so social proof transfers to new units (stop every unit relaunching
   from zero); start a Google review flow to fix the missing Google presence + lift the 8.7 Booking flank.
6. **Manage the construction/noise objection in copy** (honest disclosure + mitigation) to protect the
   4.9★ through 2026.
7. **Add missing channels:** Vrbo/Expedia, Traveloka, TripAdvisor, metasearch, luxury agencies, surf
   resellers.
8. **Unified 2-minute response layer + weekly owner report** (Villa Ops Response System) — the pre-
   arrival WhatsApp Uluwatu guests expect, and the owner visibility that's missing today.

**Keep (don't touch):** the on-ground service standard (Booking Staff 9.5, welcome-WhatsApp), the
Guesty stack, and the genuinely top-tier review ratings. Change the commercial wrapper, not the
hospitality.
