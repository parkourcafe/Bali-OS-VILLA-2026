Hard-metric comparison of the 14-villa comp set vs Anjuna. Data from public search indexing (OTAs and

Full grid in `metrics_table.csv`.

## The honest headline on OCCUPANCY (the metric you asked about)
**No competitor's occupancy % is publicly visible — for any of the 14.** AirDNA/Airbtics/AirROI do
not expose per-listing occupancy in free previews, and OTAs never show it. The only occupancy figures
that exist publicly are **market averages** and **Anjuna's own claim**:
- Uluwatu market: **~50.6% annual occupancy**, $267 ADR, **RevPAR ~$130**, ~$31k/yr revenue; villa
  segment ~62–75%; peak (Jun–Aug) up to 73%. Pecatu ~48%, ADR $247, ~$37k/yr. [TOOL — AirROI 2026]
- Top-quartile ADR ≥ $313; top-10% ≥ $470. [TOOL]
- **Anjuna's ~85%** is an operator claim (Balinest), unverified.
→ To get real per-villa occupancy for anyone (including Anjuna), you need **AirDNA/Airbtics paid pull
(~$20–100/mo)** or the **Guesty data room** (Anjuna only, via due diligence). Everything else is
market proxy. This is a genuine limit of outside analysis — not a gap in our research.

## What we CAN see, and where Anjuna stands

### Ratings & review depth — Anjuna is TOP-TIER
| Tier | Villas |
|---|---|
| 5.0 | Makai (40), **Anjuna A14 (44)**, Anjuna 2BR (6) |
| 4.87–4.89 | Bingin Castaways (129), Zyloh Seaside (106), Ayora Studio (88), **Anjuna B1 (86)** |
| 4.7–4.77 | KELUK (4.77), The Young Villas (4.74/61), Uluwatu Surf (TA 4.7/204) |
Anjuna sits in the top cluster, and its **review depth (86+44) is beaten by only Bingin Castaways,
Uluwatu Surf and Young Villas** — a real, defensible strength.

### Booking.com score — Anjuna is the WEAKEST of the scored set
Ayora 9.8 · COZ 9.3–9.6 · Marghe 9.5 · The Young Villas 9.4 · Hakuna 9.1 · Bingin Castaways 8.9 ·
**Anjuna 8.7**. Below the 9.0 "Exceptional/Superb" wall that Booking guests sort on — confirmed weak flank.

### Price — Anjuna is mid-pack but with the worst terms
High: Zyloh ~$500 · Makai $388 · COZ 3BR $430 · Palmera $249 · KELUK Dua $222 · Yin $171.
Low: **Anjuna 2BR $144** · Marghe $136 · Ayora $117 · Hakuna $66.
But Anjuna's rate is **non-refundable**, while Ayora/Marghe/COZ/Palmera offer **free cancellation** —
so Anjuna is cheap *and* rigid, the worst combination for conversion.

### Direct-booking posture — Anjuna is BEHIND most rivals (new finding)
Own booking websites/engines: COZ (cozvillas.com engine), NOUR/Amaya (Cloudbeds engine),
Zyloh, The Young Villas (+WhatsApp), Uluwatu Surf Villas, Bingin Castaways, Bukit Vista, Ayora (inquiry).
**Anjuna's direct = Guesty with a single unit indexed — one of the weakest in the set.** Most
competitors capture direct/repeat demand better than Anjuna does.

### Operator model — the set splits three ways
- **Self-operated brands with own site + direct engine:** COZ, NOUR/Amaya, Zyloh, Young Villas, Uluwatu Surf, Ayora — the strongest packagers.
- **Large third-party managers:** Bali SuperHost (Villa Yin, 300–569 villas), Bukit Vista (100+), Balinest (Anjuna, ~207).
- **Owner-operated/thin:** Hakuna Matata (no engine), Palmera/Marghe (new, thin reviews).

## Bottom line for the owner
On the numbers you CAN see, Anjuna is a **top-tier product (ratings/review depth) trapped in a
bottom-tier commercial wrapper**: weakest Booking score, non-refundable low price, and the thinnest
direct-booking presence in the set. Occupancy — the one number that would settle "are we actually
full?" — is invisible for everyone; only Anjuna's own Guesty report can prove or disprove the 85%
claim. That report is exactly what the due-diligence pack requests.
