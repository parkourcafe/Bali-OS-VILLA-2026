# Anjuna vs Competitors — Preliminary Guest-Appeal Scorecard (v0)

**What this is:** a scored ranking of the Anjuna villas against the 14-villa comp set, on the three
dimensions we can already evidence from public data. Each score is anchored to the best listing in
the set (best = 10) and carries its reason.
**What this is NOT yet:** the full 100-point score. Photos (count/quality), booking friction and
cross-channel consistency need the browser sweep — they're marked *pending*. So the total below is
**out of 30**, not 100. Directional, honest, upgradeable.

## The three evidenced dimensions
- **Positioning & title /10** — does the search-grid title sell a benefit (beach walk-time, "brand new", inclusions) or just a unit code? This is what a guest reads first.
- **Social proof /10** — rating × review *depth* (a 9.8 on 9 reviews is fragile; a 4.87 on 86 is a moat).
- **Price & value story /10** — rate vs the set + cancellation flexibility + visible inclusions.

## ➤ Anjuna diagnosis — great villa, weak listing
Anjuna lands **near the bottom (13/30) on the evidenced dimensions — despite having, in reality, one
of the best products and review records in the set.** The gap is packaging, not hospitality:

- **Positioning 2/10 (worst in set).** Titles are unit codes — "Anjuna B10", "2A5". Every rival sells
  a benefit in the title (walk-time to the beach, "brand new", "architect-designed"). A guest scanning
  the grid reads a code and moves on. *This is the single cheapest thing to fix.*
- **Social proof 7/10 (a hidden strength).** On **Airbnb this is a moat**: B1's **4.87 on 86 reviews**
  out-depths almost every new-build rival (Ayora 9, Marghe 4). The score isn't lower only because on
  **Booking the 8.7/59 sits below the 9.0+ "Exceptional" wall** the whole Pecatu grid shows.
- **Price 4/10.** $144 is low — but it's **non-refundable with no visible inclusions**, while Ayora
  ($117) and Marghe ($136) undercut from below *with free cancellation*, and Hakuna Matata wins the
  value story by bundling breakfast/wifi/transfer. Anjuna is cheap without a value narrative.

**One-line takeaway for the owner:** "Your villas out-review most rivals, but your listings sell a
unit code at a non-refundable discount — so guests pick a competitor that reads better in the same
search. Fix the packaging and the existing 5-star product converts far more of the traffic it
already gets."
