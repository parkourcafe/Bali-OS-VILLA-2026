Diagnosis

Great villa. Weak listing.

Anjuna ranks near the bottom (13/30) on the evidenced dimensions — despite having, in reality, one of the best products and review records in the set. The gap is packaging, not hospitality.

  • 2 / 10Positioning — worst in set. Titles are unit codes ("Anjuna B10", "2A5"). Every rival sells a benefit in the title — beach walk-time, "brand new", "architect-designed". A guest scanning the grid reads a code and scrolls past. The cheapest fix on this page.
  • 7 / 10Social proof — a hidden strength. On Airbnb it's a moat: B1's 4.87 on 86 reviews out-depths almost every new-build rival (Ayora 9, Marghe 4). Held back only because on Booking the 8.7/59 sits below the 9.0+ "Exceptional" wall the whole Pecatu grid shows.
  • 4 / 10Price — cheap without a story. $144 is low, but non-refundable with no visible inclusions, while Ayora ($117) and Marghe ($136) undercut from below with free cancellation, and Hakuna Matata wins by bundling breakfast/wifi/transfer.
For the owner, in one line: your villas out-review most rivals, but your listings sell a unit code at a non-refundable discount — so guests pick a competitor that simply reads better in the same search. Fix the packaging and the existing 5-star product converts far more of the traffic it already gets.
