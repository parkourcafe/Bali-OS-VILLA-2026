## Step 3 — Advocate vs Skeptic

### O1 — Villa Ops Response System
**Advocate:** The occupancy war is daily and measurable. Operators with enough portfolio volume care about direct inquiries, OTA response signals, staff load, and missed follow-up. Founder presence in Bali supports trust and in-person audits.

**Skeptic:** Smaller operators may not have enough retained management revenue for a $5,000+ system. Some sophisticated operators already have response systems. A single mystery-shop result proves only one scenario, not systemic failure. Local contracting requires partner/legal/accounting clarity.

**Resolution:** qualify for 20–60 managed villas, or minimum 15 premium villas / 30 standard villas; require meaningful direct inquiry flow and 2+ reservation handlers; use single-test wording carefully; offer smaller pilots only below threshold; complete local partner checklist before signing.

### O2 — RE Agency Lead Response (Bali)
Strong ROI story if a deal is recovered, but the sales cycle and market concentration make this backup #1 rather than the primary offer.

### O10 — Cold-Lead Revival Sprint
Fast time-to-value, but not positioned as a small down-sell from a $6,500 core package. Only use with compliant opt-out handling and confirmed database quality.

### O4 — Dubai Brokerage Follow-Up
Higher WTP but crowded, remote, and legal/privacy review required. Wave 2 after a Bali case study.

### O3 — Clinic Patient Inquiry
Good economics in some clinics, but medical guardrails make it less suitable as first wedge.
