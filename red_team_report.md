# Red Team Report — Selena Systems GTM v1.0

Severity 1–10. Fixes marked applied are reflected in the normalized package; unresolved items require founder action.

## 1. Market choice
- **Attack:** "Bali villa segment is in a downturn — you're selling buckets on a sinking ship." Severity 7.
- **Response/fix:** Data shows correction + bifurcation, not death (S17/S18). The offer is leak-repair for occupancy pressure. Applied: ICP now focuses on operators with enough portfolio volume and inquiry flow. Founder action: if audits show strong response quality across the first 15–20 qualified targets, rotate to backup offers.

## 2. Pricing
- **Attack:** "$5,000+ in Indonesia may be too high." Severity 6.
- **Fix:** Applied pricing ladder: Pilot $1,500–2,500; Core $5,000; Growth $7,500–9,500; Enterprise from $12,000. Pilot must have reduced scope and is for smaller or first-case clients.

## 3. Trust gap
- **Attack:** "No case studies, no brand, no track record." Severity 8.
- **Fix:** Applied: live demo, careful audit language, founding-client case-study exchange, no invented proof. Founder action: build demo before outreach.

## 4. Lead list quality
- **Attack:** "68 names from public sources is not a verified pipeline." Severity 6.
- **Fix:** Applied: `lead_list.csv` is now described as a research seed list. It is not a verified sales pipeline and not a list of 90+ prospects. A company can score above 90 only after real audit evidence exists.

## 5. Mystery-shop risk
- **Attack:** "One mystery-shop test can sound like entrapment or overclaiming." Severity 6.
- **Fix:** Applied wording: "In one test conducted on {date}, the response took {X}. This does not establish your normal performance, but it identified a scenario worth reviewing." Do not publish response times or describe one test as systemic proof.

## 6. Legal / local partner model
- **Attack:** "Visa, invoicing, payment receipt, data processing, and contracting may be unresolved." Severity 8.
- **Fix:** Applied: Bali sales are not automatically blocked because the founder has access to a local partner capable of supporting legal local sales and contracting. Unresolved checklist before signing: identify contracting entity; who issues invoice; who receives payment; partner fee/revenue share; delivery responsibility; data-processing responsibility; IP ownership; local legal/accounting confirmation.

## 7. Delivery complexity
- **Attack:** "WhatsApp API verification, PMS access, or client delays can break 14 days." Severity 7.
- **Fix:** Applied: the 14-day promise only applies with scope locked by day 2, WhatsApp/API access available or not critical path, max two initial channels, one availability source, no custom PMS integration, on-time KB approval, and shadow-mode rollout.

## 8. Competition
- **Attack:** "Vertical SaaS and automations are cheaper." Severity 5.
- **Fix:** Positioning is done-for-you implementation, local trust, measurable SLA, handover, and acceptance testing. Long-term defensibility remains case studies, referrals, and care plan quality.

## 9. ROI overclaiming
- **Attack:** "Using total booking value overstates ROI for management companies." Severity 7.
- **Fix:** Applied: ROI uses recovered management revenue = recovered bookings x average booking value x management commission percentage. Direct-booking commission saved, staff hours saved, and follow-up leakage can be added as estimates only.

## 10. Testing claims
- **Attack:** "The package could imply a 40-intent test suite already exists or was executed." Severity 5.
- **Fix:** Applied: the 40 scenarios are formal acceptance tests required before go-live, not tests already executed.

## 11. Founder bandwidth
- **Attack:** "One person doing sales, delivery, and support is a scaling risk." Severity 6.
- **Fix:** Documentation-first delivery and account handover reduce single-point-of-failure risk. Founder action: cap concurrent deliveries and budget for support help after client #2.

## 12. Remote strategy
- **Attack:** "Dubai cold outreach without case study is unlikely." Severity 5.
- **Fix:** Wave 2 only after Bali proof; legal/privacy review before outreach.

## Verdict
Package is now internally coherent enough for execution planning, but not for broad outreach until founder inputs are resolved. Gates: local partner/legal/accounting setup, demo agent, formal pilot scope, and audit protocol.
