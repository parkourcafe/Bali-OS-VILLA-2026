# Winning Offer — Villa Ops Response System

**AI guest inquiry, qualification and follow-up system for Bali villa management companies.**

Package version: **Selena Systems GTM v1.0**.

## Positioning
"In a market where average villas sit at 45–60% occupancy and operators are cutting prices, the operators who win are the ones who answer clearly, quickly, and consistently. Selena Systems installs the Villa Ops Response System: an AI guest inquiry, qualification, and follow-up system for Bali villa management companies. It gives eligible new inquiries on covered channels a first automated response within two minutes, qualifies the guest, follows up when conversations go quiet, and escalates anything sensitive to the team."

Not consulting. Not a chatbot subscription. A working response system, built for the client's operation, with clear scope, acceptance tests, and handover.

## Primary ICP
- Bali villa management company or multi-property rental operator.
- Ideally 20–60 managed villas.
- Minimum 15 premium villas or approximately 30 standard villas.
- At least 2 people handling reservations or guest inquiries.
- Preferably 150–300+ direct inquiry conversations per month.
- Meaningful direct inquiry flow through WhatsApp, website, Instagram, email, or direct booking.
- Internationally oriented or premium segment.
- Owner, GM, operations director, or head of reservations accessible.
- No obvious advanced internal AI response system already in place.

Companies below this threshold may qualify only for a smaller pilot.

## The painful problem (sourced)
- Guest inquiries arrive at night from AU/EU time zones; answers may come next morning, after the guest booked elsewhere. This is an industry pattern, not a per-company fact until audited (S1/S3; Dubai analog S33).
- OTA algorithms rank listings partly on response rate, so slow operators can lose visibility as well as individual guests (S14, S22).
- Communication is scattered across WhatsApp, IG DMs, email, OTA inboxes, and websites; follow-up is often manual or absent.
- Occupancy pressure and discounting make each direct inquiry more important (S17, S18).

## Commercial promise and exclusions
**SLA:** 95% of eligible new inquiries on covered channels receive a first automated response within two minutes.

Eligible inquiries are new, non-spam guest/reservation inquiries on the channels explicitly covered in the signed scope.

Exclusions: Meta, BSP, PMS, hosting, internet, or third-party outages; spam and duplicate messages; unsupported channels; messages outside the agreed automation scope; messages requiring mandatory human review; client-side access or configuration failures.

## Deliverables (fixed scope)
1. AI response agent on scoped channels, normally WhatsApp Business API and website chat, with Instagram DM only if included in scope.
2. Booking-inquiry qualification flow: dates, guests, budget, purpose, property fit, and urgency.
3. Follow-up engine for silent inquiries, using approved day-1/3/7 messaging.
4. Guest FAQ automation for check-in, directions, house rules, amenities, and basic logistics.
5. Escalation rules for complaints, emergencies, refunds, sensitive personal information, unusual requests, and mandatory human review.
6. Unified pipeline or inbox view with conversation status and ownership.
7. Response-time dashboard showing inquiries, first-response timing, handoff, and follow-up status.
8. Documentation, account handover, 60-minute team training, and 14 days post-launch tuning.

## Explicitly NOT included
- Channel-manager/PMS replacement or custom PMS integration.
- Dynamic pricing, OTA listing optimization, content, advertising, or payment processing.
- Revenue, occupancy, or booking guarantees.
- Legal/accounting setup, unless separately scoped with local advisors.

## Timeline
The 14-day delivery promise applies only when scope is locked by day 2; WhatsApp/API access is available or provisioning is not on the critical path; the first release covers a maximum of two initial channels; there is one availability source; no custom PMS integration is required; the client approves the knowledge base on time; and rollout starts in shadow mode.

For larger portfolios, additional channels, custom PMS work, or delayed access, quote a longer timeline.

## Pricing
- **Pilot: $1,500–2,500** — reduced scope for smaller or first-case clients, typically one channel, limited properties, no custom integration, and a narrow acceptance test.
- **Core: $5,000** — primary fixed-scope implementation for qualified operators.
- **Growth: $7,500–9,500** — larger portfolio, additional channel, richer dashboard or follow-up logic.
- **Enterprise: from $12,000** — multi-brand, complex routing, custom integration, or expanded governance.

Terms: 50% to start, 50% at go-live/acceptance. Client pays tool/API costs directly. Optional care plan after the included 14 days: $400–700/mo, estimate.

## ROI model (estimate only)
Do not calculate ROI using total guest booking value unless the client owns the property and retains that revenue.

For villa management companies:

**Recovered management revenue = recovered bookings x average booking value x management commission percentage**

Where applicable, also include estimated direct-booking commission saved, staff hours saved, and reduced leakage from missed follow-up. All ROI outputs are estimates, must use client-supplied inputs where possible, and must not claim that one booking automatically pays for the system unless the client's actual retained revenue supports that statement.

## Proof strategy
1. Single mystery-shop test as a sales signal, not proof of systemic failure.
2. Live demo agent on a fictional "Villa Demo Selena" the prospect can message.
3. Founding-client pilot/core offer for the first 3 clients in exchange for a written case study and referral intro.
4. Response-time dashboard becomes the client's own proof after launch.

Use this mystery-shop wording: "In one test conducted on {date}, the response took {X}. This does not establish your normal performance, but it identified a scenario worth reviewing."
