01

A WhatsApp away-message that keeps your 30-minute promise honest after hours

≈ 2 minutes · WhatsApp Business app · free

Your site promises a WhatsApp reply "within 30 minutes", but your office hours end at 17:00 (14:00 on weekends). A guest messaging at 23:00 from Europe gets silence until morning. WhatsApp Business has a built-in Away message (outside your hours) and Greeting message (first-ever contact) — set once, sent automatically. Here's text in your tone, ready to paste:

Away / greeting message
Hello, and thank you for reaching out to OriVista 🌴 (you may know us as Azure Bali — same villas, same team, new name.)

We've received your message and it matters to us. Our reservations team is currently offline, and you'll have a personal reply by 9:00 AM Bali time.

To help us come back to you faster, feel free to share:
• Which villa you're interested in
• Your dates
• Number of guests

Talk very soon,
The OriVista team
How to set it:
  1. WhatsApp Business app → Settings → Business tools → Away message
  2. Turn it on → paste the text above → schedule "Outside business hours" (set 09:00–17:00 wk / 09:00–14:00 wknd)
  3. Repeat for Greeting message (sent to anyone messaging for the first time)

Honest limit: this is a fixed auto-reply — it can't read the villa/dates, can't answer questions, and doesn't wake anyone up. It stops the silence and reassures about the rebrand; it doesn't handle the conversation. That's the gap the full system closes.

02

Stop the Azure rebrand from leaking your direct bookings

≈ 15 minutes · your web + inbox admin · free

Guests still land on book.azurebali.com, email support@azurebali.com, and see "by Azure" on OTAs — so your direct-booking traffic and reputation are split across two brands. Three concrete moves close most of the leak. Because your legacy booking pages share the same listing IDs as the new ones, the redirect is a one-liner.

A — 301-redirect the old booking domain (IDs already match)
# Netlify _redirects  (book.azurebali.com → book.orivista.com, same IDs)
/listings/*   https://book.orivista.com/listings/:splat   301!
/*            https://book.orivista.com/:splat             301!

# Apache .htaccess equivalent
RewriteEngine On
RewriteCond %{HTTP_HOST} ^(www\.)?(book\.)?azurebali\.com$ [NC]
RewriteRule ^(.*)$ https://book.orivista.com/$1 [R=301,L]
B — auto-reply + forward on the old email (until you retire it)
Thanks for writing to us! Azure Bali is now OriVista — same villas, same team. We've received your message here and our reservations team will reply from support@orivista.com shortly. For the fastest response, WhatsApp us or book direct at orivista.com. 🌴

How: in the azurebali.com mailbox settings, set this as a vacation/auto-responder and add a forward to your real OriVista reservations inbox, so nothing sent to the old address is missed while it redirects traffic.

C — ask the OTAs to drop "by Azure" from the listing names
Hi team, we've rebranded from "Azure Bali" to "OriVista" (same ownership and management). Please update our property/listing display names to remove "by Azure" and reflect "OriVista" — e.g. "Villa Kenza Canggu by Azure" → "Villa Kenza Canggu · OriVista". Property IDs are unchanged. Thank you!

How: send via each channel's partner/extranet support (Booking.com, Expedia, Airbnb, Marriott Homes & Villas, Klook, Traveloka, tiket.com, MakeMyTrip). Start with the two or three highest-volume channels.

Honest limit: redirects and auto-replies patch the leak, but they don't capture the enquiry, unify it under one pipeline, or follow up. Turning every legacy path into a tracked OriVista lead is the paid system's job.

03

A WhatsApp button that carries the villa & dates into the chat

≈ 15 minutes · your web developer · free

Today, a guest who switches from a villa page to WhatsApp starts from "Hi, is the villa available?" — your team has to re-ask everything. This button pre-fills the villa name so the chat starts with context. Drop it on each villa page (change the villa name per page):

<a class="wa-enquire"
   href="https://wa.me/628113803600?text=Hi%20OriVista!%20I%27m%20interested%20in%20Villa%20Kenza.%20My%20dates%3A%20___%20to%20___%2C%20for%20___%20guests.%20(via%20website)"
   target="_blank" rel="noopener">
  Enquire about Villa Kenza on WhatsApp
</a>
How & notes for your developer:
  1. Confirm 628113803600 is your reservations WhatsApp line (that's your published +62 811-3803-600 in international format — no +, no spaces) before shipping.
  2. Change Villa%20Kenza to each page's villa (spaces = %20).
  3. Optional: if your booking-engine villa pages have a date picker, have the developer inject the chosen dates into the text= parameter dynamically.

Honest limit: this carries context into the first message, but nobody records it, assigns an owner, or follows up — that still happens by hand across your inboxes. Capturing, routing and following up every enquiry is the paid system's job.
