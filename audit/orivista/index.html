What we found

Five observations that matter

01

The rebrand still splits your funnel across two brands

 High priority

Observed  The Azure Bali → OriVista rebrand is visibly incomplete in the places guests actually touch: support@azurebali.com still appears as a contact alongside OriVista details; book.azurebali.com listing pages are still live and indexed in parallel with book.orivista.com — the same listing IDs on both (e.g. Villa Kenza, ID 97447, on both domains); and OTA listings still carry the old name ("Villa Kenza Canggu by Azure", "Villa Casa Gypsy by Azure").

book.azurebali.com/listings/97447  =  book.orivista.com/listings/97447 (same villa, two brands) · booking.com/hotel/id/villa-kenza-canggu-by-azure.html
indexed pages · seen 2026-07-12

Guest impact: a guest who lands on an "Azure" OTA page, emails support@azurebali.com, or books on the old domain isn't sure they're dealing with OriVista at all — trust wobbles at the exact moment of purchase.

Business impact: direct-booking traffic, SEO authority and review equity are split across two brand domains instead of compounding under one. Inferred

Recommended fix: one unified enquiry front door — every legacy path (Azure email, old domain, "by Azure" OTA click) captured and answered under OriVista, with 301 redirects and an OTA-name cleanup list. Your PMS stays exactly as it is.

02

A published "within 30 minutes" WhatsApp promise with no visible meter

 High priority

Observed  Your site tells guests they can ask questions on WhatsApp and get a response "within 30 minutes", and that their preferred channel is confirmed on arrival. It's a great promise — and a concrete public SLA that currently depends on a human being awake and at a desk.

"…ask questions through WhatsApp and receive responses within 30 minutes."
indexed orivista.com content · seen 2026-07-12

Guest impact: expectations are set at 30 minutes. Any slow moment — peak season, a 2am enquiry from Europe or the US — quietly breaks a written promise.

Business impact: a premium brand carrying an unmeasured SLA. We found no public sign the 30-minute time is tracked. Not tested — actual response times need your internal data; we do not claim they are slow.

Recommended fix: an always-on first responder acknowledges every WhatsApp enquiry in seconds and logs first-response time on each one — so the 30-minute promise becomes a measured, dashboard number, safe to keep advertising, 24/7.

Screenshot slot: "within 30 minutes" WhatsApp promise (mobile)
03

Office hours end; international enquiries don't

 Medium

Observed  Your published office hours are Mon–Fri 9:00–17:00 and Sat–Sun 9:00–14:00 (Bali time). But you're listed on 8+ international OTAs — Booking.com, Expedia, Airbnb, Marriott Homes & Villas, Klook, Traveloka and more — so enquiries arrive from time zones that are wide awake when Bali's office is closed.

Office hours: Mon–Fri 9am–5pm · Sat–Sun 9am–2pm (WITA) — vs an 8+-OTA international guest base
indexed orivista.com content + OTA listings · seen 2026-07-12

Guest impact: a Sunday-evening or weekday-midnight enquirer meets the gap between "we reply in 30 minutes" and "we're open at 9am tomorrow."

Business impact: off-hours direct enquiries may wait Inferred while the OTAs that referred them confirm instantly — and take the commission.

Recommended fix: the same 24/7 responder covers nights and weekends by design, with escalation rules that wake a human only for true urgencies. The 30-minute promise holds when the office is dark.

Screenshot slot: published office hours (desktop)
04

Marketing site and booking engine are separate rooms

 Medium

Inferred  orivista.com (marketing) and book.orivista.com (a hosted booking engine — the /search + /listings/{id} pattern is most consistent with Hostaway) appear to be separate systems. We found no visible mechanism carrying villa name, dates or source from a villa page into a WhatsApp chat, so a guest who switches to WhatsApp likely starts over: "Hi, is the villa available?" — which villa, which dates, from which channel?

Not tested  Exact wa.me link behaviour and booking-engine vendor need a 2-minute check on your live pages — page HTML returned 403 to every tool this session; we'll confirm together on the walkthrough.

Business impact: your team re-asks what the website already knew; the enquiry's source and villa interest aren't captured before your PMS ever sees them.

Recommended fix: WhatsApp deep links on every villa page (both domains, during the transition) that pre-fill villa + dates + source — the chat starts qualified, and the lead is captured before handoff into your existing engine.

Review equity scattered across the old brand and OTAs

 Low
