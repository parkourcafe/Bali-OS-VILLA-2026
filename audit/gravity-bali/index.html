What we found

Five observations that matter

01

Your best-perks channel is your slowest channel

 High priority

Observed  Direct booking is rewarded with your best perks — best price, free breakfast, welcome tray. But the “Check availability” path is a form “sent directly to our marketing team” that answers with a “personalized quote” — while the same villas confirm instantly on Booking.com and Airbnb.

“Requests are sent directly to our marketing team, who will get back to you with a personalized quote.”
gravitybali.com/en/reserve-your-stay/ · seen 2026-07-12

Guest impact: a guest with two tabs open gets instant certainty from the OTA and a waiting period from you — on the channel you most want them to use.

Business impact: the direct share of revenue is capped by reply speed; latency converts into OTA commission. Inferred

Recommended fix: instant acknowledgement + qualification on every direct enquiry, 24/7. Quotes stay human-crafted — the guest just stops waiting in silence.

02

A written speed promise with no visible system guarding it

 High priority

Observed  Two separate pages promise a concierge “average response time under five minutes”, and booking requests answered “under 1 hour”.

“…average response time under five minutes…” · “…booking requests answered under 1 hour…”
gravitybali.com/en/gravity-bali-guest-experience-secrets/ · /en/bali-customer-service-key-to-rental-success/ · seen 2026-07-12

Guest impact: expectations are set at five minutes. Any slow moment — peak season, night-time enquiries from Europe or Australia — breaks a written promise.

Business impact: a premium brand carrying an unmeasured SLA. We found no public sign that reply times are tracked. Not tested — actual response times require your internal data; we do not claim they are slow.

Recommended fix: measure first-acknowledgement time on every enquiry; make the median a dashboard number. The promise becomes auditable — and safe to keep advertising.

Screenshot slot: “<5 minutes” promise (mobile)
03

“7 days a week” vs “closed on weekends” — on the same website

 High priority

Observed  The Conciergerie page advertises a dedicated WhatsApp concierge “7 days a week”, while your guest FAQ states the concierge “is closed on weekends — for urgent requests, contact your villa manager”.

“The concierge service is closed on weekends. For urgent requests, contact your villa manager directly.”
gravitybali.com/en/faqs-for-guests/ vs /en/gravity-conciergerie/ · seen 2026-07-12

Guest impact: a Saturday enquirer doesn’t know whether to expect five minutes or Monday. Weekends are ≈28% of the week — and the moment many guests plan trips.

Business impact: weekend direct enquiries may queue until Monday Inferred — while OTAs answer them instantly.

Recommended fix: a 24/7 first responder covers weekends by design, with escalation rules for true urgencies; the copy contradiction is a 5-minute content fix we’ll flag precisely.

Screenshot slot: FAQ weekend note + Conciergerie “7 days” (side by side)
04

Context likely dies between the villa page and WhatsApp

 Medium

Inferred  The form flow and the WhatsApp flow appear disconnected: we found no mechanism that carries villa name, dates or source into a WhatsApp chat. A guest switching channels starts over: “Hi, is the villa available?” — which villa, which dates, from where?

Not tested  Exact WhatsApp link behavior needs a 2-minute check on your live pages — we’ll do it together on the walkthrough.

Business impact: your team re-asks what the website already knew; the enquiry’s source and villa interest go unrecorded.

Recommended fix: WhatsApp deep links on every villa page that pre-fill villa + dates + source — so the chat starts qualified.

05

Five inboxes, no single pipeline

 Medium

Inferred  Enquiries can arrive via the reserve form (a mailbox), villa-page forms, WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, OTA threads and email. We found no public trace of a unified pipeline. If that matches reality, then: no single owner per lead, no follow-up cadence, no source attribution — and no way for you to see the whole funnel.

Business impact: “Why didn’t they book?” is currently unanswerable. Follow-up depends on memory in six different places.

Recommended fix: one pipeline for every channel — statuses, owners, reminders, source stamps, and a weekly funnel view for you both.
