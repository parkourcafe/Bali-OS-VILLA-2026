## Why OriVista (fit rationale)

1. **A concrete, demonstrable headline problem.** The Azure→OriVista rebrand is
   visibly incomplete in guest-facing places (legacy email, parallel booking
   domain, "by Azure" OTA names). This is *in scope, publicly checkable, and
   fixable in 14 days* — the strongest kind of audit finding.
2. **A published SLA to protect.** "WhatsApp within 30 minutes" is a public
   promise dependent on human staffing — exactly what an always-on first
   responder makes measurable and safe.
3. **A structural off-hours gap.** Office hours end 17:00 (14:00 weekends) for
   an 8+-OTA international guest base.
4. **Affordability.** 52 villas, physical office, India back-office with a
   dedicated Revenue Manager, hosted PMS, 8+ OTA channels, trade-show presence
   → a $5,000–9,500 one-off project is comfortably affordable.

## The critical positioning nuance (read before writing anything)

OriVista **already runs a booking engine and their own copy touts "AI
automation."** Therefore:

- **Do NOT** pitch "you have no booking system" (Gravity's angle). That's false
  here and will end the conversation.
- **DO** pitch the **pre-booking WhatsApp enquiry capture + qualification +
  follow-up + brand-unification layer** that sits *around* their PMS. Frame
  Villa Ops OS as **augmenting** their stack.
- **Objection pre-empt ("we already have AI"):** lead with the concrete,
  measurable, *publicly visible* gaps their internal automation demonstrably
  hasn't closed — the rebrand leaks, the unmeasured 30-min SLA, the off-hours
  coverage. Internal automation is invisible from outside; the leaks are not.

## Watch-outs

- Data-savvy, hypergrowth founder (0→52 in 2 yrs) may lean DIY — the gift page

  pipeline + measured SLA they *haven't* built.
