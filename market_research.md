# Market Research Summary — Selena Systems

Research date: July 2026. Sources: see source_log.md (S-numbers below refer to it). Assumptions labeled.

## 1. The core insight

Across every market researched, the same expensive failure repeats: **businesses pay to generate inquiries, then lose them to slow, chaotic, WhatsApp-scattered response handling.**

- Real estate: the average agent takes ~15 hours to respond to a new lead (S4), while responding within 5 minutes makes a lead 21x more likely to qualify (S1, S3). 62% of inquiries arrive after hours (S1). 78% of buyers work with whoever responds first (S1).
- Clinics/medspas: small clinics miss 30–40% of peak-hour calls (S25); up to 85% of unanswered callers never call back (S29); an average medspa visit is worth $500–700 (S28).
- Bali villa operations: "slow to respond" and "WhatsApp-only communication" are documented red flags owners are warned about (S9); top operators differentiate on <60-minute response (S10); OTA algorithms rank listings partly by response rate (S14).
- Dubai brokerages: a documented case of an 8-agent agency losing 43 warm leads/quarter through a shared WhatsApp inbox — ≈604,800/yr in potential commission (S35).

This is the pain Selena Systems sells against. It is measurable (mystery-shop test), expensive (one lost deal > project fee), and fixable in a 14-day sprint.

## 2. Bali market condition — the strategic pivot

The founder's on-the-ground read ("villa sales are struggling") is partially confirmed by data:

- The market corrected ~5% then stabilized at a $299k median; transactions grew +6.4% YoY — a maturing market, not a dead one (S16, S18).
- The real distress is in **rentals**: oversupply pushed average villas from ~80% historical occupancy to 45–60% in shoulder season (S17); operators are cutting rates 10–30% (S18); 45,774 active listings compete, 85% concentrated in 1–3BR (S23).
- The market has bifurcated: professionally managed, legally compliant, fast-responding properties win; the rest enter a "revenue death spiral" as OTA algorithms deprioritize them (S22, S14).

**Implication:** in a contracting/competitive market, each lead is worth MORE, and operators fighting an occupancy war have urgent, daily pain. Therefore:

- **Segment #1 (primary): Bali villa management companies & rental operators** — fighting for occupancy, direct bookings, and OTA ranking. Daily pain, owner-decision-maker, founder can meet in person.
- **Segment #2: Bali/intl real estate agencies still transacting** (Canggu corridor = 33.5% of sales, S16) — the speed-to-lead ROI story; one saved commission pays 2–4x the fee.
- **Segment #3 (hedge/backup): Bali aesthetic clinics** — medical tourism from AU/SG grows independently of the villa market (S48 directory: 51 clinics); missed-inquiry economics documented (S25–S32).
- **Segment #4 (remote hedge): Dubai brokerages** — 32,294 brokers (S34), WhatsApp-native, documented lead leakage, deal sizes that dwarf the fee. Fully remote-sellable.

## 3. Bali-local vs remote vs hybrid — recommendation

**Hybrid, sequenced: Bali-first for the first 1–3 clients, Dubai-remote as the parallel second track.**

Why Bali first for speed:
- Founder presence removes the cold-outreach trust gap: walk-ins, coffee meetings, expat founder networks.
- Target businesses are English-speaking, expat-owned or international-facing (assumption A9), concentrated in Canggu/Berawa/Pererenan/Seminyak/Uluwatu.
- Mystery-shop audit is executable tonight at zero cost and produces personalized proof.
- Local win → local case study → referrals in a tight community.

Why Dubai in parallel (not instead):
- Bigger absolute market and deal sizes; buying automation is already normalized there (S33–S38 are all vendors selling into that demand — proof of market).
- But: crowded vendor field, no in-person edge, cold-trust barrier without case studies. Better as wave 2 armed with a Bali case study.

Why not the others first: Singapore/Australia (procurement friction, compliance, no edge), Shopify e-commerce (saturated by cheap SaaS chatbots at $50–200/mo — hard to defend $6.5k), agencies/creators (can build automations themselves; price-sensitive).

## 4. Competitive landscape

- Generalist AI automation agencies price single-workflow builds at $5–15k (S43) or $1.5–5k at the low end (S39); typical retainers $1–7k/mo (S40, S42). **Selena's $5–10k fixed sprint is squarely market-normal.**
- Vertical SaaS exists in every segment (PropSpace, Ruby CRM, PatientNow/Recura, Hyperleap, PropPilot...). Their weakness: software-only, self-serve or sales-team onboarding, generic scripts, no on-the-ground implementation, no accountability for the client's actual response time. **Selena's wedge: done-for-you implementation + a measurable promise (first response <5 min on every channel) + local presence.**
- Most competitor marketing is stat-heavy hype. Selena differentiates with calm, evidence-first positioning and the mystery-shop audit as the opener.

## Selena Systems GTM v1.0 normalization notes

The normalized primary offer is **Villa Ops Response System**: AI guest inquiry, qualification and follow-up system for Bali villa management companies.

The primary ICP is narrowed to Bali villa management companies or multi-property rental operators with enough inquiry volume and retained revenue to justify implementation: ideally 20–60 managed villas; minimum 15 premium villas or approximately 30 standard villas; at least 2 people handling reservations or guest inquiries; preferably 150–300+ direct inquiry conversations per month; meaningful direct inquiry flow through WhatsApp, website, Instagram, email, or direct booking; internationally oriented or premium segment; accessible owner/GM/operations/reservations decision maker; and no obvious advanced internal AI response system already in place. Companies below this threshold may qualify only for a smaller pilot.

`lead_list.csv` is a research seed list with 68 named companies. It is not a verified sales pipeline and not a list of 90+ prospects. Per-company pain remains unverified until audit evidence exists.

The consistent commercial promise is: 95% of eligible new inquiries on covered channels receive a first automated response within two minutes. Exclusions: Meta, BSP, PMS, hosting, internet, or third-party outages; spam and duplicate messages; unsupported channels; messages outside the agreed automation scope; messages requiring mandatory human review; client-side access or configuration failures.

ROI for management companies must estimate retained management revenue, not total guest booking value unless the client owns the property and retains that revenue. Formula: recovered management revenue = recovered bookings x average booking value x management commission percentage. Where applicable, add estimated direct-booking commission saved, staff hours saved, and reduced leakage from missed follow-up.
