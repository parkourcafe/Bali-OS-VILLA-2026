# Public Effectiveness Audit — method

How to judge, from public data only, how well a Bali villa-management company fills its villas and
markets itself. Reusable on any prospect. Nothing here needs their cooperation — it's OSINT from
OTAs, socials, and search. Use it to (a) qualify prospects, (b) build a credible outreach hook, and
(c) benchmark competitors.

Rule: record only what is publicly visible, with the source. Proxies are signals, not proof — label
them as estimates.

## What "effectiveness" means here
Two questions:
1. **Occupancy / demand** — are the villas actually getting booked (загрузка)?
2. **Marketing & reputation** — is their advertising and brand converting into bookings and trust?

## The signals (and where to read them)

### A. Distribution & reach
- Which OTAs list them: Airbnb, Booking.com, Agoda, VRBO, Expedia, Trip.com, Marriott Homes & Villas.
- How many distinct listings on each.
- Direct-booking engine in use (Guesty, Hostaway, Lodgify) — check the booking-site URL / page source.
- **Reads as:** breadth of demand capture + tech maturity.

### B. Occupancy proxies (загрузка) — no one publishes real occupancy, so infer it
- **Review velocity** — total reviews ÷ months since first review = bookings/month proxy. High velocity = high turnover = high occupancy.
- **Calendar availability** — open a listing, check next 30/60 days: many blocked dates = booked (or off-market). Compare weekdays vs weekends.
- **Airbnb badges** — "Guest favorite", "Rare find", Superhost, "booked X times", min-night rules.
- **Booking.com scarcity** — "booked X times in 24h", "only N left".
- **Reads as:** an occupancy estimate. Cross-check velocity + calendar to avoid being fooled by a new or dormant listing.

### C. Reputation (отзывы)
- Rating + review count on each channel: Airbnb, Booking, Google Business Profile, Agoda, Tripadvisor.
- **Sentiment themes** — read the newest 15–20 reviews: what's praised (location, staff, cleanliness) vs complained about (communication, maintenance, check-in).
- **Owner responses** — do they reply to reviews, and how fast/professionally?
- **Reads as:** service quality + operational discipline. Communication complaints = direct opening for our pitch.

### D. Responsiveness (this is our product's whole thesis)
- Airbnb "Response rate" and "Responds within an hour/day" on the host profile.
- WhatsApp/IG DM: send one real pre-booking question and time the reply (respectful one-test mystery-shop; log date/time; one test ≠ systemic proof).
- **Reads as:** how leaky their first-response is — the exact gap we sell into.

### E. Marketing effectiveness (реклама)
- **Instagram** — followers, post count, posting cadence (posts/week), engagement rate (avg likes+comments ÷ followers). 1–3% is healthy; <0.5% on a big following = weak.
- **Paid ads** — Meta Ad Library (facebook.com/ads/library) → search the page name → are ads running, how many, what creative/offer? Google: does their name trigger Google Ads?
- **Website** — quality, clear booking CTA, SEO: do they rank for "Uluwatu villa rental", "Canggu villa management"? Similarweb for a traffic-tier estimate if available.
- **Reads as:** are they buying demand or earning it; is spend converting.

### F. Pricing & positioning
- Nightly rates (ADR) from the listings; compare to similar villas in the same area/bedroom count.
- **Reads as:** premium vs value position; pricing power = demand strength.

## Turn signals into a verdict
Score each 1–5 (or High/Med/Low) and write one honest paragraph:
- **Demand/occupancy:** strong / mixed / weak — from B.
- **Reputation:** strong / mixed / weak — from C.
- **Responsiveness gap:** big / some / small — from D (this sizes our opportunity).
- **Marketing:** earning demand / buying demand / underinvested — from E.
- **Net:** is this company effective, and where's the leak we can fix?

## Caveats
- Review velocity undercounts (not every stay leaves a review) and can't separate villas that went off-market — use it as a floor, cross-checked with the calendar.
- Automated fetchers get 403 on many Bali sites/OTAs; some reads need a real browser or the mobile app.
- One mystery-shop test is a signal, not proof. Keep it respectful and single.
