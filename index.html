The core mechanism

A response system, not a chatbot

An AI layer installed over the channels you already use. It answers from your approved knowledge base only — and when it isn't sure, it hands off to a human instead of improvising.

01 · Inquiry

A guest writes at 11pm

WhatsApp, website chat — whichever channels are in your signed scope. Time zone doesn't matter anymore; AU and EU guests inquire while Bali sleeps.

02 · Response

Answered within two minutes

Accurate, in the guest's language, from information you approved: properties, ranges it may quote, house rules, logistics. Never an invented rate — that guardrail is contractual.

03 · Qualification

Serious inquiries get shaped

Dates, guests, budget, purpose, property fit, urgency — collected conversationally, so your team wakes up to a qualified lead instead of a cold "hi, price?"

04 · Follow-up

Silence gets a second chance

Conversations that go quiet are followed up on day 1, 3, and 7 with messaging you approved. The inquiries you already earned stop evaporating.

05 · Handoff

Humans take what humans should

Complaints, emergencies, refund requests, sensitive personal information, unusual requests, and anything you mark as mandatory human review — escalated instantly with full context. And there's always a pause-AI procedure.

FAQ

Fair questions, straight answers

Will it say something wrong to a guest?

It answers only from the knowledge base you approve. Unknown pricing, unavailable dates, discount requests beyond your approved range — those are among the adversarial scenarios in the acceptance suite, and the correct behavior is to route to your team, not to improvise. There is also a documented pause-AI procedure your staff controls.

We already have auto-replies.

Auto-replies acknowledge. This system answers, qualifies, follows up on day 1/3/7, and hands off with context. The difference shows up in your pipeline, not your greeting message.

What if we use a PMS or channel manager?

We can read from simple availability sources where available. Custom PMS integration is explicitly not part of the 14-day scope — if you need it, we'll say so and quote it separately rather than pretending it fits.

Who owns the system afterwards?

You do, subject to final contracting and account setup: accounts are created in your name wherever possible, documentation and credentials are handed over, and the included training covers editing the knowledge base yourself.

What does ROI actually mean here?

For management companies, ROI is estimated from retained management revenue — recovered bookings × average booking value × your management commission — plus staff hours saved. Not total guest booking value, unless you own the property and keep that revenue. All ROI figures are estimates built from your own inputs, never guarantees.

What happens during an outage — Meta, hosting, internet?

Third-party outages are excluded from the SLA — we can't promise Meta's uptime — but they're not ignored: outage behavior, escalation failure, and human takeover are tested scenarios in the acceptance suite, and the documented failure mode routes inquiries to your team rather than silence.

Do you have case studies?

Not yet — and we won't invent any. That's why the readiness assessment is free, the demo agent is public, prices are published, acceptance is a formal 40-scenario test you witness, and founding clients get Pilot/Core pricing in exchange for a written case study they approve.

Is my guests' data safe?

Guest data is processed only to operate the system, in accounts you own. Sensitive personal information is a mandatory human-review category by default. Data-processing responsibility is confirmed in the contracting checklist before signing.

Yours to keep: the accounts, the documentation, the proof.
