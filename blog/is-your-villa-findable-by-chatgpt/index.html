AI search readiness

Is your villa findable by ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google's AI?

Guests used to type into a search box. Increasingly they ask an assistant: "Where should I stay in Uluwatu for a week with a private pool?" ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google's AI answer by reading and quoting websites they can access. If yours is hard for them to read, you're invisible in exactly the moment a guest is deciding. Here's what those assistants can and can't see on a villa site — and how to become the one they recommend.

Can AI assistants even reach your site?

The first gate is access. AI crawlers — GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended and others — read your robots.txt before anything else. Plenty of villa sites, often without realising it, block these crawlers. If yours does, you can't be suggested no matter how lovely the property. Being reachable is the price of being recommended.

Can they read your content, or does it hide behind JavaScript?

Many modern sites render their real content with JavaScript after the page loads. Search and AI crawlers often take the first version of the page — and if that version is nearly empty, your rooms, rates and location simply aren't there to read. If the meaningful text on your villa pages only appears after scripts run, assistants usually skip it.

Are your details machine-readable (structured data)?

When your location, room count, price range and FAQs are written only into your visual layout, a machine has to guess. Structured data — JSON-LD — states those facts plainly in a format built for machines. It doesn't change what guests see; it makes your details easy to quote accurately, which is precisely what an assistant does when it answers a guest's question about your area.

Do you answer the questions guests actually ask?

Assistants love pages that answer real questions directly. A clear FAQ — "Is the villa suitable for families?", "How far is the beach?", "What's included?" — written as questions and straight answers (and marked up as an FAQ) is exactly the kind of content that gets quoted. It also happens to reassure human guests.

Do you have an llms.txt?

An llms.txt is a short plain-text file at your site root that tells AI assistants, in a friendly way, what you offer and where the key pages are. It's the newest of these signals and still rare among villa sites — which makes it an easy way to get ahead. (This very site publishes one at /llms.txt.)

The honest caveat

None of this guarantees an AI will recommend you — no one can promise that, and anyone who does is bluffing. What it does is remove the reasons you'd be skipped: blocked crawlers, unreadable content, missing details. You can't win a race you're not visible in.
